the idea

見当Kentō — the mark that makes every print identical.

Before a colour is printed, the carver cuts kentō registration marks into the block: an L-shaped kagi at one corner and a straight hikitsuke along an edge. The paper is seated against them for every pass, so a dozen blocks land in perfect alignment — the first impression and the hundredth, the same.

That is Hayao's promise, drawn from craft rather than jargon: same seed, same build → the same result, every run. Determinism is a registration mark for software. The identity is built on that one honest rhyme — quiet, exact, and made by hand.

the mark

A registration mark, not a logo of a thing.

The mark is the kentō itself: the vermilion kagi and hikitsuke holding the corner of an implied sheet. It is abstract and non-linguistic by design — a printer's tool, never a character.

wordmark

Mincho, set quietly, with room to breathe.

The wordmark is a single Mincho line — high-contrast, brush-cut strokes — with the mark to its left. On ink, it reverses to washi. Give it clear space equal to the height of the mark on every side.

colour

Traditional pigments, used with restraint.

Three inks and a paper. Vermilion (shu) is a seal, not a fill — it appears only on the mark and a single accent per view. The colours carry their historical Japanese names.

duty of care

Homage is earned, not worn.

This identity borrows a living craft. It is built to read as respect — and the same restraint that keeps it respectful is what keeps it elegant. Two rules do most of the work: credit the technique, and abstract, never counterfeit.

Before any public use

Do
  • Credit kentō and the ukiyo-e lineage on the about page.
  • Keep every mark abstract and non-linguistic.
  • Commission a paid review from a Japanese designer with authority to say no.
  • Use real colour names accurately (藍 ai, 朱 shu, 墨 sumi).
Don't
  • Invent kanji, or set text in Japanese you can't read.
  • Claim exactness the artwork doesn't hold — it is seigaiha-inspired.
  • Imply any tie to a filmmaker or studio. The name is evocative, standalone, not an endorsement.
  • Fill large fields with vermilion, or crowd the layout. Ma is the point.
