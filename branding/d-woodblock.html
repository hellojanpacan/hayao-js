Woodblock

A woodblock is a deterministic function: carve it once, and every impression it prints is identical — the first and the ten-thousandth.

The idea

The name is Japanese, and the engine's deepest promise — same inputs, same output, forever — is exactly the promise of the ukiyo-e printmaker: one carved block yields perfectly reproducible impressions. Direction D borrows that craft language — sumi ink, indigo, a single vermilion seal (hanko) as the "verified" mark, seigaiha waves — as a genuine conceptual rhyme for determinism, not decoration.

When this is right — and the duty of care

Choose Woodblock for the most distinctive, story-rich identity: it fuses the Japanese name, the craft heritage, and the determinism thesis into one metaphor no competitor can copy.

Considerate use is mandatory. This borrows from a living culture, so it must read as homage, not costume: authentic geometry (real seigaiha, real seal proportions), no kanji we can't stand behind, credit the ukiyo-e lineage in the about page, and ideally a review by a Japanese designer before launch. Done carelessly it becomes pastiche — that risk is the price of the payoff.
